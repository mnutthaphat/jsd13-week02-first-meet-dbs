## 1. Describe the human problems
During exam seasons (especially midterms and finals), university students often struggle to find study groups or qualified tutors. One-on-one tutoring is highly expensive (averaging 400 THB/hour), creating a heavy financial burden for students. Furthermore, it is difficult to find tutors who genuinely understand and excel in the specific subjects taught at their particular faculty or university.

## 2. Describe how your solution solves these problems
Our platform is a university-level tutoring website focused on "small group learning and pay-per-session" to solve the problem of expensive tuition. Students can post requests for specific subjects, specify their available times, and attach study materials in advance. The system groups students together (2-4 people) to split the cost (reducing the individual fee from 400 THB/hr for 1-on-1, down to 100-200 THB/hr per person as more friends join), while the tutor still earns their minimum rate of 400 THB/hr.

To ensure safety and quality, the platform strictly requires tutors to verify their identity using their National ID, Student ID, and transcripts showing their grades in those specific subjects. To prevent users from taking classes off-platform, we feature an in-built Live Video and Screen Sharing system (no need for external apps like Zoom), along with an upfront deposit system to prevent cancellations or no-shows from either party.

## 3. Describe your ecommerce business idea including core features
- User registration, login, and identity verification (KYC system using National ID, Student ID, and academic transcripts)
- Student Request Board (A system for students to post tutoring requests, specify dates/times, and attach study materials)
- Sliding Scale Pricing System (Dynamic pricing that decreases per student as more join: 2 students = 200 THB/hr, 3 students = 150 THB/hr, 4 students = 100 THB/hr)
- Deposit and Payment Gateway (A secure payment and deposit system prior to class to prevent no-shows)
- In-built Live Video & Screen Sharing (Native live classroom on the website; no external apps required)
- Commission Management (Automated system that deducts a 15% platform fee from the tutor's earnings per session)
- Search and filter tutors/requests (Search by university, faculty, and specific subjects)
- Review and Rating System (Post-session rating system to build trust and tutor credibility)
- Notifications for class schedules and matchmaking (Alerts for accepted jobs and upcoming classes)

## 4. Market Size & Estimated Minimum Monthly Revenue
**Baseline Data for Estimation:**
- Thailand has approximately 150 universities (let's estimate 30 highly active/competitive universities).
- Each university averages 15 faculties / Each faculty has about 10 highly difficult subjects that often require tutoring.
- Total potential subjects = 4,500 subjects.

**Minimum Estimated Monthly Revenue (Regular Season):**
- Assuming only 50 classes occur per day (or 1,500 classes/month).
- 1 class requires a minimum of 2 hours = Tutor earns 800 THB.
- The platform deducts 15% = 120 THB / class.
- **Platform Revenue = 1,500 x 120 = 180,000 THB / month.**

**Estimated Monthly Revenue (Peak Season: Midterms/Finals):**
- Class volume could spike to 300 classes/day (9,000 classes/month).
- The platform deducts 15% = 120 THB / class.
- **Platform Revenue = 9,000 x 120 = 1,080,000 THB / month.**

## 5. Additional Business Ideas
- **Marketplace for Study Notes:** Allow top-performing students to sell their summary sheets as PDF files (watermarked to prevent piracy). The platform takes a 20-30% cut, creating passive income for both the site and the creators.
- **Record Class Feature:** A feature to record the live tutoring sessions, allowing students to rewatch them for 7-14 days (a small premium fee could be charged for access to recordings).
- **Bounty / Matchmaker System:** If a student posts a request but no tutor accepts, they can add a "Bounty" (extra money) to incentivize top-tier tutors to take the job.
- **Party Leader Promotion:** The student who initiates the request ("opens the party") could receive a slightly larger discount than the friends who join later, incentivizing users to actively create new class requests.
